# Contribute

We welcome contributions of all kinds, including bug reports, code, documentation, design ideas, and use cases.

## Getting Started

General contribution information can be found in our [Contributing to AboutCode](https://aboutcode.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing.html) documentation.

Specific contribution guidelines for our major projects are available in their respective repositories:

*   **ScanCode Toolkit**: [CONTRIBUTING.rst](https://github.com/aboutcode-org/scancode-toolkit/blob/develop/CONTRIBUTING.rst)
*   **VulnerableCode**: [CONTRIBUTING.rst](https://github.com/aboutcode-org/vulnerablecode/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.rst)
*   **ScanCode Workbench**: [CONTRIBUTING.rst](https://github.com/aboutcode-org/scancode-workbench/blob/develop/CONTRIBUTING.rst)

## Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO)

To ensure we can use your contributions, we require that you agree to the **Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO)**. This is the same mechanism used by the Linux Kernel project.

By signing off on your commits, you certify that you have the right to submit your code under the project's license.

## Code of Conduct

We are committed to fostering an open and welcoming environment. Please review our [Code of Conduct](https://aboutcode.org/code-of-conduct) before participating.
